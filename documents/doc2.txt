-Use chests in boats in the end for fighting the dragon, it can't delete them as they are entities, not blocks
-Place slabs or stairs in the water rows of your farms. You have no risk to fall in water and jump up on your crops this way
-An oak tree placed in a 2 deep hole almost always grows into a big tree

-Doors are a good way to restrict arthropod mobs and give breathing pockets underwater
-A lightning rod still works when floating in the sky
-Carefully remove the amethyst and calcite around budding amethyst to max out your ROI on shards
-Cobblestone wall is superior to fencing for fences and windows, but not if it goes up to the ceiling. Slab your ceilings
-Placing an overhanging roof necessitates placing a two high stair/fence wall near or on the ground to keep out mobs.
-Endermen prefer to teleport near you than right next to you, you can use this to your advantage in cramped areas.

- Creating villager librarians and breaking the lectern over and over again until they will trade you the enchanting book you need most is way overpowered. (And a bit tedious). Early Fortune III and Mending for the win, so you don't need that many diamonds. Combine with an XP farm and you can get really good gear early on with little risks taken.

- If you bring villagers to your base and make job blocks or beds for them, it's all too easy to trigger a raid in your base.

- While caving may get you progress fast, strip mining and avoiding them gives you consistent resources with low risk, and a lot of building blocks to build stuff with.

- A lot of mobs that survive daylight still need darkness to spawn. Lighting up the area around your base goes a long way to keeping you safe. For instance, illager patrols only spawn in low light.

- Always add torches to one side of a cave when moving inwards, so if you end up exploring a labyrinth, you can find your way out by following the path where the torches are on the other side.
